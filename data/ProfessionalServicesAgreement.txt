Professional Services Agreement — Condensed Version (100 Lines)

Agreement Structure
This Agreement includes the Cover Page Key Terms, SOW, and Common Paper Professional Services Standard Terms v1.1. If conflicts exist, the Cover Page or SOW controls.

SOW Overview
Each SOW defines Services, Deliverables, timelines, milestones, Fees, and Customer Obligations.

Services
Provider will perform Services as described in the SOW using its own tools and personnel.

Cooperation
Customer will reasonably cooperate with Provider to enable Service delivery.

Change Orders
Either party may propose changes; modifications require written approval.

Acceptance Process
Deliverables are accepted unless rejected within the Rejection Period, after which Provider may resubmit corrections.

Subcontractors
Provider may use Subcontractors with permission and remains responsible for their work.

Customer Obligations
Customer will fulfill responsibilities outlined in the SOW.

Deliverable Ownership
Except for Pre-Existing Materials and Third-Party Materials, Deliverables are assigned to Customer at the Time of Assignment.

Customer Materials
Provider may use Customer Materials only to perform Services.

Pre-Existing Materials
Provider grants Customer a limited license to embedded pre-existing IP required to use Deliverables.

Third-Party Materials
Third-party content may be used only if permitted in the SOW and properly licensed.

Feedback and Usage Data
Provider may use Feedback and aggregated Usage Data to improve services.

Reservation of Rights
Each party retains ownership of its intellectual property unless expressly transferred.

Privacy and Security
Both parties will comply with any Data Processing Agreement and Security Policy.

Fees
Fees may be hourly, milestone-based, or project-based as defined in the SOW.

Invoices
Provider will invoice according to the Invoice Period.

Payment Terms
Customer will pay invoices within the Payment Period.

Taxes
Customer pays applicable taxes except Provider’s income taxes.

Payment Disputes
Customer must notify Provider of disputes during the Payment Period and pay undisputed amounts.

Agreement Term
The Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues until 12 months after the last SOW ends.

Termination for Cause
Either party may terminate for uncured breach, insolvency, or non-curable violations.

Force Majeure Termination
Either party may terminate if uncontrollable events prevent Services for extended periods.

Termination Without Active SOWs
Either party may terminate if no SOWs remain active.

Effect of Termination
Services stop, licenses end where applicable, confidential information must be returned or destroyed, and final invoices issued.

Survival Clauses
IP rights, payment obligations, confidentiality, liability limits, and general terms survive termination.

Representations and Warranties
Each party confirms legal authority and compliance with laws.

Provider Warranties
Provider will perform Services professionally and Deliverables will meet SOW requirements.

Customer Warranties
Customer confirms rights to provide materials and third-party content.

Warranty Remedy
Provider will reperform Services if Deliverables fail to meet requirements; termination and refund may apply if unresolved.

Disclaimer of Warranties
Except for stated warranties, Services and Deliverables are provided “as is.”

Limitation of Liability
Liability is limited to the agreed cap except for Increased or Unlimited Claims.

Damages Waiver
Neither party is liable for indirect or consequential damages unless prohibited by law.

Indemnification by Provider
Provider will defend Customer against Provider Covered Claims.

Indemnification by Customer
Customer will defend Provider against Customer Covered Claims.

Indemnification Procedures
Protected parties must provide notice, cooperation, and defense control.

Insurance
Parties will maintain required insurance policies if specified.

Confidentiality
Confidential information must be used only for Agreement purposes and protected with reasonable care.

Confidentiality Exceptions
Public or independently developed information is excluded.

Required Disclosure
Confidential information may be disclosed when required by law with advance notice where allowed.

Permitted Sharing
Confidential information may be shared with advisors bound by confidentiality obligations.

Entire Agreement
This Agreement replaces prior agreements regarding Services.

Modifications
Changes must be written and accepted by both parties.

Governing Law and Courts
Disputes will be resolved under the selected jurisdiction.

Injunctive Relief
Parties may seek court orders to prevent IP or confidentiality violations.

Assignment
Rights cannot be assigned without consent except during mergers or asset transfers.

Publicity
Neither party may publicly reference the Agreement without approval unless allowed by Key Terms.

Notices
All legal notices must be sent to the designated addresses.

Independent Contractors
The parties act independently and are not partners or agents.

No Third-Party Beneficiaries
Only Provider and Customer have rights under this Agreement.

Force Majeure Protection
Delays caused by uncontrollable events do not create liability except for payment obligations.

Export Compliance
Services and Deliverables must comply with export control laws.

Anti-Bribery
Both parties must follow anti-corruption regulations.

Electronic Signatures
The Agreement may be signed electronically or in counterparts.

Definitions
Defined terms include Affiliate, Deliverables, Services, Change Order, and Confidential Information.

Customer Materials Definition
Materials provided by Customer for use with Services.

Pre-Existing Materials Definition
Provider IP created outside the Agreement.

Third-Party Materials Definition
IP owned by external parties used within Deliverables.

Usage Data Definition
Aggregated performance or usage data related to Services.

SOW Definition
A document outlining specific project terms under the Agreement.
