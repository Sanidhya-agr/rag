Partnership Agreement — Condensed Version (100 Lines)

Agreement Structure
This Agreement includes the Cover Page Business Terms and Key Terms plus the Common Paper Partnership Standard Terms v1.1. If conflicts exist, the Cover Page controls.

Obligations
Company and Partner will perform the promotional, referral, payment, or branding activities listed in the Cover Page.

Territory
The partnership applies worldwide unless a specific geographic region is defined.

Payment Process
Invoices and payments will follow the process and contacts listed in the Cover Page.

Payment Schedule
Any Fees owed will be paid according to the agreed schedule.

Term and End Date
The Agreement starts on the Effective Date and ends after the specified duration or custom end date.

Governing Law and Courts
The Agreement is governed by the chosen laws and disputes must be filed in the designated courts.

Cooperation
Each party will perform its Obligations in good faith.

Feedback
Either party may give Feedback, which may be used freely by the receiving party.

Fees and Billing
Fees are in U.S. Dollars unless stated otherwise and exclude taxes.

Payment Responsibility
The paying party must pay invoices and applicable taxes according to the Payment Schedule.

Taxes
Each paying party covers sales, VAT, or similar taxes but not the other party’s income tax.

Trademark License
Each Licensor grants the other party a limited, non-exclusive license to use Brand Elements only to fulfill Obligations.

Ownership of Brand Elements
Each party retains ownership of its trademarks and branding.

Restrictions on Branding Use
Brand Elements cannot be modified, misused, or used outside the Agreement scope.

Approvals
Licensor may review and approve uses of its Brand Elements.

Privacy and DPA
If a Data Processing Agreement exists, both parties must comply with it.

Escalation Procedure
Disputes must first be addressed through written notice and good-faith discussions before legal action.

Termination Rights
Either party may terminate for uncured breaches, insolvency, or serious violations.

Force Majeure
Either party may terminate if uncontrollable events prevent performance for extended periods.

Effect of Termination
All branding licenses end, confidential information must be returned or destroyed, and final invoices must be settled.

Survival Clauses
Payment, confidentiality, IP rights, liability limits, and general terms survive termination.

Representations and Warranties
Each party confirms legal authority, compliance with laws, ownership of branding, and proper handling of personal data.

Disclaimer of Warranties
Except for stated warranties, all services and obligations are provided “as is.”

Limitation of Liability
Liability is limited to the agreed cap unless exceptions apply.

Damages Waiver
Neither party is liable for indirect or consequential damages except where prohibited by law.

Exceptions to Liability Limits
Certain claims such as confidentiality breaches or indemnification may have higher or unlimited caps.

Indemnification by Company
Company will defend Partner against Company Covered Claims.

Indemnification by Partner
Partner will defend Company against Partner Covered Claims.

Indemnification Process
The protected party must provide notice, cooperation, and defense control to the indemnifying party.

Exclusive Remedy
Indemnification obligations define the full liability for Covered Claims.

Confidentiality Obligations
Confidential information may only be used for Agreement purposes and must be protected with reasonable care.

Confidentiality Exceptions
Public, independently developed, or legally obtained information is not confidential.

Required Disclosure
Confidential information may be disclosed if required by law with advance notice when allowed.

Permitted Sharing
Confidential information may be shared with employees or advisors bound by confidentiality obligations.

Entire Agreement
This Agreement replaces all prior agreements relating to the subject matter.

Modifications
Changes must be written and signed by both parties.

Injunctive Relief
Parties may seek court orders to prevent IP or confidentiality breaches.

Assignment
Rights may not be assigned without consent except during mergers or business transfers.

Notices
Legal notices must be sent to the specified notice addresses.

Independent Contractors
The parties are independent contractors and not partners or agents.

No Third-Party Beneficiaries
Only Company and Partner have rights under this Agreement.

Force Majeure Protection
Delays caused by uncontrollable events do not create liability except for payment obligations.

Anti-Bribery
Both parties must comply with anti-corruption laws.

Interpretation
Section titles are for convenience only and do not affect meaning.

Electronic Signatures
The Agreement may be signed electronically or in counterparts.

Definitions
Key terms such as Affiliate, Brand Elements, Confidential Information, Feedback, Fees, and Covered Claims apply throughout.

Brand Elements Definition
Includes logos, trademarks, marketing assets, and related materials.

Confidential Information Definition
Includes non-public information shared under the Agreement.

Force Majeure Definition
Includes disasters, war, pandemic, or infrastructure failures beyond reasonable control.
